Polarized vs UV400: What Actually Matters?

Understand lens labels so you can buy based on protection, not just marketing language.

5 min read Eye Care Basics Lens Guide

UV400 Is Non-Negotiable

UV400 means lenses block almost all UVA and UVB rays. For outdoor use, always choose UV400 regardless of lens color.

What Polarization Adds

Polarized lenses reduce glare reflected from flat surfaces like roads, glass, and water. They improve contrast and reduce eye strain while driving or traveling.

When You Need Both

Best everyday setup is UV400 + polarization. You get radiation protection plus visual comfort in bright environments.

Buying Checklist

  • Confirm UV400 label.
  • Check for polarization if you drive often.
  • Pick lens tint based on use: gray for neutral color, brown for contrast, green for comfort.
